inequality of health care in the disabled community

Accessibility to healthcare in the disabled community is a rising issue. Some doctors have refused to treat people with disabilities due to their lack of ability. Doctors have not been trained to work with a diverse demographic. It is time to properly train these doctors and physicians. People with disabilities deserve the same care as any other person.

About

While this issue might not affect you directly, it's affecting people around you. Take a step back and recognize your privilege; are you able to walk into a doctors office and receive care? That is a privilege. People in the disabled community do not have the opportunity or accessibility to receive proper treatment. From a very young age I have worked with kids apart of the disabled community. It has truly changed my life. I have learned so much over the years. There are multiple stereotypes in the disabled community. Even in a school setting they are treated differently. Most of them have a para with them, walking them everywhere. Not only that, but they have their own classrooms and their own electives. This excludes them from ever having a normal high school experience. They are not able to develop friendships when they are isolated. This eventually leads to misconceptions about the disabled community.People look down on them, misjudge them, and discriminate against them. This is just as promante in our healthcare system. Doctors have denied care to people with disabilities before due to their own insecurities and lack of training. This is why we are fighting for the disabled community to get equal accessibility to healthcare. It is time to properly train doctors and physicians to handle diverse situations

What is Disability Discrimination?

This video is about what disability discrimination is actually defined as. Disability descrimination is explained in this video as the discrimination of an individual based on the disabilities or physical/ mental impairment they may suffer from. This video explains that as long as your disability or impairment is long term, 12 months of suffering or more, and prevents you from doing normal everyday activities then you will be protected from being discriminated against based on this disability.

What's Disability to Me?

This video is about Bernard, a person with disabilities that participates in life completely. Speaking in front of people, travelling, and fighting for what he believes in. People with disabilities still want to be independent in making choices and not be put in institutions.

Mark's Story

This video shows a video of a man named, Mark Riffle, who acts as a standardized patient in a wheelchair. A "standardized" patient is someone who will test doctors and other medical professionals on how they will treat a real patient. Mark's goal is to make sure people with disabilities, such as himself, are treated just like other patients. Mark reccounts times in the past when he was not treated fully by doctors because of his disability

'I Am Not The Doctor For You': Physicians' Attitudes About Caring For People With Disabilities

Click here to open this PDF in a new window

The article talks about how people with disabilities face multiple barriers when attempting to gain access to health care. Doctors do not have the communication or knowledge to treat the disabled community. To increase access to health care in the disabled community, it will require increasing the accessibility of space and the availability of equipment. The article talks about conducting a research experiment to see how much physicians and doctors really know. Each physician in the experiment took accountability for the lack of knowledge. This article shows the readers how misjudged the diabled community is. Many physicians during the experiment had a negative view towards the disabled community and showed ableist language. This really shows how behind we are as a society in educating peers and professionals about the disabled community. The article relates to the picture because the picture shows how someone with a disability could be treated in a healthcare setting. The doctors do not know how to work with certain disabilities.
